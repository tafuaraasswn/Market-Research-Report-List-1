<p><p>The intracranial pressure monitoring devices market is categorized into invasive and non-invasive monitoring devices. Invasive monitoring devices involve the insertion of sensors into the skull to provide real-time pressure readings, offering high accuracy and sensitivity, often used in critical care settings. Non-invasive monitoring devices utilize techniques like imaging or external sensors to assess intracranial pressure without direct penetration. While less accurate, they provide safer alternatives for continuous monitoring in various clinical scenarios, appealing to broader patient demographics.</p></p>
