<p><p>3D Arterial Spin Labeling (3D ASL) is a non-invasive MRI technique that measures cerebral blood flow. The market includes two primary types: Continuous Arterial Spin Labeling (CASL) and Pulsed Arterial Spin Labeling (PASL). CASL, characterized by continuous labeling of arterial blood, offers high signal-to-noise ratios and better perfusion mapping, making it suitable for high-resolution imaging. In contrast, PASL employs short labeling pulses, providing flexibility and efficiency, especially for research and clinical applications that require rapid imaging.</p></p>
