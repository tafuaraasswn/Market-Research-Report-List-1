<p><ul><li>DC Microgrid</li><li>AC Microgrid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Independent microgrid markets can be categorized into DC and AC microgrids, each serving distinct applications. DC microgrids operate on direct current, benefiting renewable energy sources like solar and batteries, offering efficient energy storage and distribution for localized systems. Conversely, AC microgrids utilize alternating current, which is compatible with traditional grid infrastructure, making them suitable for a wider range of applications, including residential and commercial setups. Both types enhance energy resilience, enable integration of renewable resources, and support energy autonomy.</p></p>
