<p><p>Cryopreservation equipment in the stem cell market operates primarily in two phases: liquid phase and vapor phase. </p><p>Liquid phase systems involve submerging biological samples directly in liquid nitrogen, providing consistent and rapid cooling, ideal for long-term storage. In contrast, vapor phase systems use nitrogen vapor to cool samples above the liquid, reducing contamination risks and allowing for easier sample handling. Both methods are essential for preserving viability and functionality of stem cells, playing a crucial role in regenerative medicine and research applications.</p></p>
