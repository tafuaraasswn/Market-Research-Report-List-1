<p><p>The arthroscopy shaver market is segmented into monopolar and bipolar types, each serving distinct surgical needs. Monopolar shavers use a single electrode to cut and coagulate tissue, ideal for procedures requiring extensive tissue removal. In contrast, bipolar shavers employ two electrodes to facilitate cutting and coagulation simultaneously, offering more precision and reduced thermal damage. The choice between monopolar and bipolar shavers influences surgical outcomes, efficiency, and recovery, catering to varying requirements in orthopedic and sports medicine surgeries.</p></p>
